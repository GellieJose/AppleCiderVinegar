How to Choose the Best Apple Cider Vinegar

When selecting the best apple cider vinegar (ACV), it's important to consider a few key factors to ensure you're getting the highest quality product. Here’s what to look for:

  • Raw and Unfiltered:Raw and Unfiltered: The best ACV should be raw and unfiltered. This ensures that it still contains the "mother" — strands of proteins, enzymes, and beneficial bacteria that provide health benefits. You can usually spot this at the bottom of the bottle.
  • Organic:Opting for organic apple cider vinegar ensures that the apples used were grown without pesticides or synthetic fertilizers, making it a healthier choice for both your body and the environment.
  • Glass Bottle:ACV should ideally be stored in a glass bottle rather than plastic. Glass preserves the integrity of the vinegar and prevents leaching of harmful chemicals that could be present in plastic.
  • Acidity Level:Most apple cider vinegars have an acidity level of around 5%. Ensure that your vinegar’s acidity matches the intended use, whether it’s for drinking, cooking, or skincare.
  • Reputable Brands:Choose brands that are well-known for their quality, such as Bragg, which is a trusted option and widely available. Checking the product's reputation through customer reviews can also give you an idea of its quality.
